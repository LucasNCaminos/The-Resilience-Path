# Training Write-up: ISC2 - Certified in Cybersecurity (CC)

## Phase I: Foundational Security Domains & Applied Knowledge

This phase documents the technical and strategic mastery of the five core domains of the ISC2 CC curriculum. My approach was to treat each domain not as a theoretical requirement, but as a functional toolset to be applied in my current professional environment at **Fourier** and in my personal labs.

### 1. Security Principles
* **The CIA Triad in ERP Systems:** I have moved beyond the definition of Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability. In my daily work with **TOTVS Protheus**, I apply these principles by auditing user permissions (Confidentiality), validating SQL entry points (Integrity), and ensuring system uptime through process optimization (Availability).
* **Authentication & Non-Repudiation:** Implementation of logical controls to ensure that every action within a financial environment can be traced back to a verified identity.

### 2. Business Continuity (BC), Disaster Recovery (DR) & Incident Response (IR)
* **Applied Strategy:** During my training, I focused on the difference between BC and DR. I’ve applied this by analyzing the "Recovery Time Objectives" (RTO) for critical ERP modules. 
* **IR Simulation:** I practiced the six-step incident response lifecycle through tabletop exercises, simulating a data breach scenario to understand how to contain threats without disrupting the entire business flow.

### 3. Access Control Concepts
* **Least Privilege & Zero Trust:** I've applied the **Principle of Least Privilege** in my role as a consultant, identifying and removing "Privilege Creep" in client databases.
* **Defense in Depth:** Mastering the layering of physical, administrative, and technical controls to create a resilient security architecture.

### 4. Network Security
* **Pillar of Trust:** Training on ports, protocols (TCP/IP vs. OSI), and the importance of securing network devices (Firewalls, IDS/IPS). I utilized this knowledge to better understand the communication between ERP clients and database servers.

### 5. Security Operations
* **Log Management & Hardening:** Understanding the lifecycle of data and the importance of centralized logging for forensic analysis.

## Phase II: Strategic Reflection & Professional Utility

### Why ISC2 CC?
I chose to undergo the ISC2 CC training because I believe that technical skill without a strategic framework is incomplete. This certification path provided me with the "Global Language" of cybersecurity. It taught me that security is not just about blocking an IP address; it’s about understanding the **risk to the business**. 

### Utility & Professional Growth
I found this training extremely useful. It gave me the vocabulary and the mindset needed to communicate with C-level executives. It allowed me to transform from a "technician who fixes systems" to a "security-minded architect who protects assets." It adds a layer of formal maturity to my profile that complements my technical background in engineering and network administration.

### Personal Disclaimer: The "Skills-First" Path
> **Transparency Note:** I have completed the entire official training and successfully passed all practice evaluations for the ISC2 CC. Due to current economic prioritization, I have not yet sat for the official proctored exam. However, I have chosen to prioritize the **immediate application of this knowledge** in real-world scenarios. In my view, the value of a security professional is measured by their ability to defend an environment, and this training has given me the tools to do exactly that. I am ready to prove this knowledge in any technical challenge or professional environment.
